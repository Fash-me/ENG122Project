The current cybersecurity landscape is characterized by many threats, including phishing, ransomware, malware, and data breaches. According to recent reports, cyberattacks have become more frequent and sophisticated, targeting organizations of all sizes and sectors. For example, the healthcare industry has experienced a surge in ransomware attacks, jeopardizing patient data and disrupting essential services (Neprash et al., 2022). Attacks like this can make patients hesitant to share their personal information or seek medical care if they perceive healthcare organizations as unable to protect their data effectively. Similarly, financial institutions have faced significant losses due to data breaches and fraudulent activities (Cremer et al., 2022). When customer's identities are stolen and they have no idea it was stolen, cybercriminals can open bank accounts with that information and they will launder money through those accounts or deposit fake checks, which will cause financial institutions to lose their money. These incidents underscore the urgent need for effective cybersecurity measures.

While government regulations exist to address cybersecurity concerns, their effectiveness has been questioned. Current regulatory frameworks vary across jurisdictions and sectors, leading to inconsistencies and gaps in cybersecurity enforcement (Cremer et al., 2022). Additionally, compliance with existing regulations can be challenging and costly for businesses, particularly small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). As a result, many organizations struggle to implement adequate cybersecurity measures, leaving them vulnerable to cyber threats.

Stricter government regulations are necessary to strengthen cybersecurity protections and safeguard individuals, businesses, and critical infrastructure sectors. Comprehensive regulations can establish clear standards and requirements for cybersecurity practices, helping organizations better protect against cyber threats. Moreover, regulations can promote information sharing and collaboration among stakeholders, facilitating the development of best practices and threat intelligence sharing (Greiman, 2015). By mandating cybersecurity training and awareness programs, regulations can empower individuals to recognize and respond to cyber threats effectively (Nifakos et al., 2021).
